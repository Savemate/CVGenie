South African Workers Rights & Protections

Know your rights under South African labour law and find help when you need it

Your Rights Under South African Labour Law

The South African Constitution and labour legislation provide comprehensive protection for all workers. Key legislation includes the Basic Conditions of Employment Act, Labour Relations Act, Employment Equity Act, and Occupational Health and Safety Act.

Fair Remuneration

  • National Minimum Wage (R27.58 per hour from 2024)
  • Overtime pay (1.5x normal rate)
  • Public holiday pay (2x normal rate)
  • Equal pay for work of equal value
  • Timely payment of wages

Safe Working Conditions

  • Occupational Health and Safety Act compliance
  • Right to refuse dangerous work
  • Protective equipment provision
  • Regular safety training
  • Health and safety representatives

Non-Discrimination

  • Employment Equity Act protections
  • No discrimination based on race, gender, pregnancy, marital status, ethnic or social origin, colour, sexual orientation, age, disability, religion, conscience, belief, political opinion, culture, language, birth or HIV status
  • Reasonable accommodation for disabilities
  • Harassment-free workplace

Reasonable Working Hours

  • Maximum 45 hours per week (9 hours daily if 5-day week, 8 hours daily if 6-day week)
  • Rest breaks: 60 minutes after 5 hours
  • Daily rest: 12 consecutive hours
  • Weekly rest: 36 consecutive hours
  • Annual leave: 21 consecutive days
  • Sick leave: 30 days over 3 years
  • Maternity leave: 4 months

Freedom of Association

  • Right to join/form trade unions
  • Right to collective bargaining
  • Right to strike (with proper procedures)
  • Protected disclosure (whistleblowing)
  • No victimization for union activities

Fair Labour Practices

  • Written particulars of employment
  • Procedural fairness in dismissals
  • Notice periods: 1 week (under 6 months), 2 weeks (6-12 months), 4 weeks (over 12 months)
  • Severance pay: 1 week per completed year
  • Protection against unfair dismissal

Common Workplace Issues & Solutions

What to do: Keep pay slips and work records. First approach your employer in writing. If unresolved, visit your nearest Labour Centre or call the Department of Employment and Labour at 0860 666 663.

Time limit: 3 years from date payment was due

What to do: Refer the dispute to the CCMA within 30 days of dismissal. Complete Form 7.11. The CCMA will attempt conciliation. If unsuccessful, the matter may go to arbitration.

Contact CCMA: 0861 16 16 16

What to do: Document incidents with dates, witnesses, and evidence. Report to employer first. If unresolved, contact the Commission for Employment Equity or the CCMA.

Time limit: 6 months from date of incident
